WHAT IS A COMBINE?

Where discipline meets opportunity.

This is not just a camp. It is where young athletes learn what it takes to lead. Built on grit, discipline, and hometown pride, WCFCC develops champions who carry those lessons for life.

A combine is an event designed to develop athletic skill, discipline, confidence, and leadership through structured training and evaluation.

FOOTBALL

Athletes work on drills, fundamentals, positioning, and game readiness. Each session builds the skills needed to compete with confidence and improve measurably throughout the camp.

CHEER

Athletes focus on technique, form, strength, coordination, and performance confidence. Each practice develops the foundation needed to perform as part of a team with energy and precision.

CHEER CAMP

INTRODUCTION

The WCFCC Summer Cheer Camp is a recreational, football sideline focused cheer camp designed to build confidence, teamwork, and strong fundamentals.

Athletes focus on sideline cheers, motions, jumps, formations, and age appropriate stunting with clear expectations for each age group.

This is not an all star or competition cheer camp.

CAMP PHILOSOPHY

This camp emphasizes structure, encouragement, and skill development in a positive environment.

Athletes are taught how to perform together as a team, stay engaged on the sidelines, and grow at their own pace while learning proper technique and timing.

AGE APPROPRIATE STUNTING

Stunting is introduced safely and progressively based on age, readiness, and group coordination.

Athletes focus on proper flyer body positions, timing, technique, and communication within structured stunt groups.

CORE SKILL AREAS

SIDELINE CHEERS

Chants and cheers for game day energy

MOTIONS & TIMING

Sharp technique and synchronized execution

JUMPS

Performance quality, leadership, and consistency.

Skills emphasized:

Sideline cheers • Jump sequences • Dance choreography • Stunting based on experience • Leadership and accountability

This group moves at a faster pace and prepares performance material earlier in the camp.
