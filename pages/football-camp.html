Camp Philosophy

This camp emphasizes structure, discipline, and skill development in a competitive yet supportive environment.

Athletes are taught fundamental techniques, game strategy, and teamwork while building confidence and leadership skills on and off the field.

What is a Combine?

A combine is a structured athletic evaluation designed to measure and develop key performance metrics through standardized drills and testing.

Athletes participate in drills that test speed, agility, strength, and position specific skills. Each drill provides measurable data that tracks improvement throughout the camp.

Combine Drills Include:

  • • 40 Yard Dash: Measures straight line speed
  • • 20 Yard Shuttle: Tests lateral quickness and change of direction
  • • 3 Cone Drill: Evaluates agility and body control
  • • Broad Jump: Measures explosive lower body power
  • • Vertical Jump: Tests vertical explosion
  • • Position Specific Drills: Evaluates technique and fundamentals for each position

Week 1: Athletes establish baseline measurements

Week 4: Athletes are re-evaluated to track measurable improvement

Game Day Fridays

Every Friday, camp moves to our turf stadium for live 7 on 7 competition.

7 on 7 Format:

Non-contact scrimmage focusing on passing game, route running, coverage, and game strategy. Athletes apply the week's training in live competitive situations.

  • ✓ Quarterback decision making under pressure
  • ✓ Receiver route precision and separation
  • ✓ Defensive back coverage techniques
  • ✓ Game situation awareness

Why This Matters

Most athletes finish summer camp and never see their coaches or teammates again. WCFCC is different.

The post camp practices give you ongoing skills training and coaching as you transition to your fall teams. Need help with footwork? Want to sharpen your routes? Coaches are there to help you stay game ready.

Plus, you're part of something bigger. Walking in front of 70,000 people wearing WCFCC colors builds pride, brotherhood, and memories that go way beyond one summer.
