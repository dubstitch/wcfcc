8-11

Introduction

The WCFCC Summer Cheer Camp is a recreational, football sideline focused cheer camp designed to build confidence, teamwork, and strong fundamentals.

Athletes focus on sideline cheers, motions, jumps, formations, and age appropriate stunting with clear expectations. Camp also includes learning performance routines for the Hagerstown Mummers Parade in October.

This is not an all star or competition cheer camp.

Camp Philosophy

This camp emphasizes structure, encouragement, and skill development in a positive environment.

Athletes are taught how to perform together as a team, stay engaged on the sidelines, and grow at their own pace while learning proper technique and timing.

Beyond summer, our squad stays connected through October as we prepare for the Alsatia Mummers Parade, Hagerstown's biggest annual event with 70,000+ spectators.

Game Day Fridays

Every Friday, cheerleaders perform at our turf stadium during live 7 on 7 football games.

What to Expect:

Cheerleaders perform sideline cheers, execute formations, and bring energy to support the football teams during live competition.

GLOW

Ages 8 to 11

Learning how to cheer as a group, perform on the sidelines, and build confidence through structured training.

Skills emphasized:

Sideline cheers • Basic motions and timing • Jump basics • Formations and spacing • Ground based visual stunts • Parade choreography • Performance presence

No elevated stunting. Emphasis on confidence, coordination, teamwork, and learning to perform in front of crowds.

BEYOND SUMMER: MUMMERS PARADE

OCTOBER 31, 2026 | DOWNTOWN HAGERSTOWN

WCFCC doesn't end when summer camp ends. We take our squad to the biggest event in Hagerstown: the Alsatia Mummers Parade with 70,000+ spectators watching.

Perform your routines on our custom float, walk the parade route, and represent WCFCC with pride.

What Cheerleaders Do

  • ✓ During Summer Camp: Learn routines that will be performed at the parade, help design and start building the WCFCC float
  • ✓ Post Camp Practices (3x/month): Perfect your routines, practice formations, work on timing, continue building the float
  • ✓ Parade Day: Perform choreographed routines on the WCFCC float and walk the parade route in front of 70,000+ spectators

Why This Matters

The Mummers Parade is the biggest stage in Hagerstown. This is your moment to shine in front of thousands of people, cameras, and judges.

The post camp practices keep your skills sharp, build squad unity, and ensure everyone is confident and synchronized for the big performance.

This isn't just another performance. It's the culmination of everything you worked on during summer camp, and it's a memory you'll carry forever.
