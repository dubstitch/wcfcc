CURRICULUM
The Journey

CHEER
CURRICULUM

Every rep builds toward the next Friday performance. Athletes progress through four weeks of sideline cheers, motion technique, choreography, jumps, teamwork, and showmanship.

1

FOUNDATIONS

Cheer voice, motion placement, counts, jump prep, and first sideline cheers.

2

SIDELINE READY

Game day cheers, formations, jump sequence, sharper motions, and crowd confidence.

3

ROUTINE BUILD

Stunt and pyramid basics, choreography sections, team timing, and routine transitions.

4

SHOWCASE

Championship Day performance, full routine, sideline confidence, and team celebration.

Divisions

AGE GROUP
EXPECTATIONS

Two cheer divisions, same program path. Each group trains at the right pace for their age, confidence, and skill level.

Foundations division. Athletes ages 8–10 build cheer voice, motion placement, beginner jumps, rhythm, and confidence on the sideline.

Skills emphasized: Cheer voice & projection · Motion sharpness · Jump basics · Simple choreography · Formations · Confidence.

Performance division. Athletes ages 11–13 sharpen technique, choreography, timing, jumps, and performance presence.

Skills emphasized: Advanced motion combinations · Jump sequence · Routine execution · Crowd engagement · Synchronization · Showmanship.
