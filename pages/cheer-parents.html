Cheer · Parent Info

CHEER
PARENTS

Everything parents need to know about schedule, drop-off, what to bring, weather, illness, and our code of conduct.

Weekly Rhythm

CAMP
SCHEDULE

Tuesdays

6:00 — 8:00 PM

Practice. Cheers, motions, jumps, choreography.

Thursdays

6:00 — 8:00 PM

Practice. Routine work, conditioning, formations.

Fridays

6:00 — 8:30 PM

Game Day at the Turf Stadium. Tailgate encouraged.

Drop Off & Pickup

EVERY
PRACTICE

Arrival

Plan to arrive 15 minutes early. Coaches are on-site 30 minutes before practice for setup.

Pickup

Be prompt. Practice ends at 8:00 PM (8:30 on Fridays). Late pickups disrupt the next thing on the schedule.

What To Bring Daily

  • Water bottle
  • Sunscreen
  • Athletic shoes (sneakers or cheer)
  • Hair ties & bobby pins
  • Positive attitude

Game Day Attire

Camp uniform, pom-poms, hair accessories (bow / scrunchie), athletic shoes. Tailgate gear — chairs, snacks, family — welcome.

Day To Day

FOOD,
WEATHER, HEALTH

Food & Hydration

Eat before arriving. Water and Gatorade provided. Light snacks at every practice. Snooks Shaved Ice & Soft Serve caters one weekday + every Friday. Friday game days include a full meal.

Facilities

No bathrooms during weekday practices — use the restroom before arriving. The Turf Stadium has full facilities on Fridays.

Weather Policy

We operate rain or shine. Cancellations are decided 2 hours before practice and pushed via email and text. Extra water breaks and shade time during extreme heat.

Illness & Injury

Keep sick athletes home. Minor injuries get on-site first aid. Serious injuries: parent contact + 911 if needed. All staff are CPR & First Aid certified.

Family

PARENT
INVOLVEMENT

Watching Practice

Optional — you’re welcome anytime. Some kids focus better when parents aren’t watching. Do what works for your athlete.

Friday Tailgates

Encouraged! Bring chairs, food, the whole family. Friday is a celebration of the week.

Championship Day

The biggest day of camp. Full 2.5-minute routine, awards, team photos. Plan to be there.

Communication

Weekly emails for updates. Text alerts for last-minute changes. Coaches available before and after practice for quick questions.

Code of Conduct

SHARED
EXPECTATIONS

Athletes

  • Be punctual
  • Be respectful to coaches and teammates
  • Give full effort
  • Take care of your gear
  • Lift up the team — unity over ego

Parents

  • Avoid sideline coaching during practice
  • Communicate respectfully with staff
  • Model sportsmanship at game days
  • Read weekly emails
  • Honor pickup times
