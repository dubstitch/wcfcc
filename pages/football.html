Testing

COMBINE
EXPLAINED

A combine is a structured athletic evaluation that measures speed, agility, power, and position specific skills. Athletes test in Week 1, train through the program, and re-test in Week 4 to track improvement.

COMBINE DRILLS

◆40 Yard Dash — Measures straight line speed.
◆20 Yard Shuttle — Tests lateral quickness and change of direction.
◆3 Cone Drill — Evaluates agility and body control.
◆Broad Jump — Measures explosive lower body power.
◆Vertical Jump — Tests vertical explosion.
◆Position Specific Drills — Evaluates technique for each position.

TESTING TIMELINE

WEEK 4

Divisions

AGE GROUP
EXPECTATIONS

Three football divisions, same training path. Each group develops at the right pace for their age, confidence, and game experience.

Foundations division. Athletes ages 9 to 11 learn competitive football basics with emphasis on fundamentals, teamwork, and confidence.

Skills emphasized: Field positions · Ball handling · Basic offensive and defensive concepts · Safe contact progressions · Team communication.

Development division. Athletes ages 12 to 14 build stronger technique, faster decision making, and better understanding of game strategy.

Skills emphasized: Position specific drills · Route running · Blocking fundamentals · Defensive reads · Play execution · Conditioning basics.

Performance division. Athletes ages 15 to 17 sharpen high school readiness through advanced technique, leadership, and competitive execution.

Skills emphasized: Advanced game strategy · Position mastery · Leadership · Situational awareness · Competitive conditioning · Football IQ.
